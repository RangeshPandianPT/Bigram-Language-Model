The Olympus Mons Base, established in 2042, is the first permanent human settlement on Mars. It is located at the foot of the tallest volcano in the solar system. The base currently houses 150 scientists and engineers.
The primary energy source for the base is a combination of advanced solar panels and a next-generation micro-nuclear reactor known as the "Ares Reactor". Water is extracted from subsurface ice deposits using autonomous drilling rovers.
The most significant discovery made by the Olympus Mons Base team occurred in 2045, when they found fossilized microbial life in the Valles Marineris canyon system, definitively proving that Mars once harbored life.
